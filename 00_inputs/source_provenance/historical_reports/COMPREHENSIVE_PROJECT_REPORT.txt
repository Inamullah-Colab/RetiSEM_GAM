SECTION 5: VARIABLE REDUCTION & DATASET OPTIMIZATION
================================================================================

MULTICOLLINEARITY-DRIVEN VARIABLE REMOVAL:

Based on the 32 highly correlated pairs identified, applied domain knowledge
to systematically remove redundant variables while retaining maximum information.

VARIABLES REMOVED (20 total) & RATIONALE:
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. CHOLESTEROL MEASURES (keep base, remove SI units):
   ✗ LBDTCSI - Duplicate of LBXTC (r=1.000, identical data)
   ✗ LBDHDDSI - Duplicate of LBDHDD (r=1.000, identical data)
   Rationale: SI units are mathematical conversions, not independent data

2. WEIGHT & BODY COMPOSITION (keep BMI, remove components):
   ✗ BMXWT - Raw weight (r=0.901 with BMI, and r=0.922 with arm measure)
   ✗ BMXWAIST - Waist circumference (r=0.885 with BMI)
   ✗ BMXARMC - Arm circumference (r=0.899 with arm cuff, r=0.863 with waist)
   ✗ BMXARML - Arm length (r=0.870 with height, redundant body dimension)
   ✗ BMXCALF - Calf circumference (r=0.896 with thigh)
   ✗ BMXTHICR - Thigh circumference (r=0.895 with calf)
   ✗ BPACSZ - Arm cuff size (r=0.899 with arm circumference)
   Rationale: BMI is composite measure; other body measures add multicollinearity
             without substantial independent information for causal pathway

3. BLOOD PRESSURE READINGS (keep 1st reading, remove 2nd & 3rd):
   ✗ BPXSY2 - 2nd systolic BP reading (r=0.868 with 1st reading)
   ✗ BPXSY3 - 3rd systolic BP reading (r=0.842 with 1st reading)
   ✗ BPXDI2 - 2nd diastolic BP reading (r=0.811 with other readings)
   ✗ BPXDI3 - 3rd diastolic BP reading (multiple high correlations)
   ✗ BPXML1 - Arm length for BP cuff (r=0.910 with systolic, measurement artifact)
   Rationale: Multiple readings measure same construct; 1st reading is standard
             in epidemiology; additional readings add collinearity without new info

4. AGE MEASURES (keep years, remove derived measures):
   ✗ RIDAGEMN - Age in months (r=0.998 with years, derived from years)
   ✗ RIDAGEEX - Age at exam (r=0.997 with months, derived from years)
   Rationale: All are derived from single age value; years is standard unit

5. SOCIOECONOMIC VARIABLES (keep household, remove family):
   ✗ INDFMINC - Family income (r=0.945 with household income)
   ✗ DMDFMSIZ - Family size (r=0.943 with household size)
   Rationale: Household measures are primary; family measures redundant

6. LANGUAGE VARIABLES (keep one):
   ✗ AIALANG - Language indicator (r=0.920 with other language variable)
   Rationale: Remove duplicate language classification

7. SURVEY WEIGHTS (keep intake, remove MEC):
   ✗ WTMEC2YR - MEC exam weight (r=0.999 with intake weight)
   Rationale: Nearly identical weights; intake weight is more standard
